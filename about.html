About Eid al-Adha

Eid al-Adha (Festival of Sacrifice) is the most significant of two Islamic Eids, observed on the 10th day of Dhul-Hijjah. It honors Prophet Ibrahim's (AS) devotion and willingness to sacrifice his beloved son at Allah's command, before Allah replaced his son with a ram as a sign of divine mercy.

Muslims around the world commemorate this spirit of obedience and gratitude through prayer, charity, and the sacrifice of livestock, sharing the meat with family, friends, and the less fortunate.

  • Obedience: Demonstrates submitting to Allah's will.
  • Charity: Distributes blessings among the poor.
  • Unity: Celebrates unity among the ummah.

Eid al-Adha is one of the holiest days, coinciding with the rites of Hajj in Makkah, and reminds all of sacrifice, compassion, and faith.
